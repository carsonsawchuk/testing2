Airplane

I helped design and manufacture an airplane which was partially 3D printed with a group of students on campus. We created a flyable airplane that could be maneuvered mid-flight. I specifically designed the moving wing mechanism, which allowed the plane to be able to turn, gain, and loose elevation at the users control. The plane was created in SOLIDWORKS. This project involved teamwork as communication with students to align our vision and our models was vital in building a cohesive plane.

Electric Car Frame

Over the summer I started an electric go-kart project so I could learn more about electric vehicles. I utilized SOLIDWORKS 3D sketching to build a car frame and test it using FEA analysis. This ensured that when I did build the frame it would be strong enough to withstand driving forces. This model was also used to ensure that all components would fit within the frame.

Built Car Frame

After designing the car frame in SOLIDWORKS, I built it. This involved cutting metal at precise angles and lengths so that everything would fit together nicely. I learned skills such as welding when putting the car frame together. After this, the next step is to finalize the electronics for the vehicle so that the battery pack, speed controller, and motor can be connected.

Arduino Pattern Detector

This project was used to learn more about the capabilities of TOF sensors and see what applications they can be used for. I used two TOF sensors, one to measure the distance of the front face and another to measure the deviations in the pattern. By doing this, I could then use MATLAB code to graph the surface and determine what the pattern was on the outside surface.

Test Stand

This test stand was designed in SOLIDWORKS and then manufactured using resin printing techniques. The stand had to be able to have an adjustable middle part where electricity could flow through and adjustable side arms for varying piece sizes. Tests could then be conducted on this stand.

ANSYS

I utilized ANSYS Fluent to calculate the drag coefficient and pressure forces when air was flowing around the nose cone at Mach speed. This was used to determine where weak points were within the nose cone so design changes could be made to ensure a stable rocket flight and to guarantee a strong nose cone that wouldn’t fail during flight.

Hovercraft

With a team, I built a hovercraft that would was able to run for over 10 minutes. This involved doing many calculations ensuring that the fans we chose could provide ample lift for the hovercraft. The hovercraft had to go twenty-five feet down a course and then turn autonomously. This was done by using weights to shift the direction of airflow and thus change the direction of the hovercraft.

Parachute Deployer

This model was a parachute deployment system module that could be put into a rocket. It included two canisters that would contain CO2 cartridges and black powder where the black powder, once detonated, would force the cartridge up into a spike, releasing the gas, and separating the nosecone and allowing for the parachute to be deployed. This was designed for both the drogue parachute and the main parachute.
